About Aleksander Kravchenko

I was born in the Kharkiv region of Ukraine. I am married and we have a child.
I graduated from: Kharkiv National University of Urban Economy. By specialty: Electrical power engineering, electrical engineering and electromechanics

My main hobby is cars — their history, development, tuning, and car audio. I really enjoy classic cars. I inherited one from my grandfather, and I personally restored and repaired it, driving it on weekends. For me, it’s like a Lego set for adults.

My first profession was auto mechanic, and I started my career working in that field. Later, I changed jobs after receiving a good financial offer from a construction company, where I began working as a manufacturer of reinforced concrete structures. I proved myself well and eventually became a team leader, supervising several employees and managing the production of elevator shafts, structural walls, and columns in apartment buildings.

While working there, I also enrolled in university to study Electrical Power Engineering, as I planned to grow within the construction company. However, due to the financial crisis of 2008–2009, construction in Ukraine nearly stopped, and I had to look for a new job.

My passion for cars helped me, and I found a job as a car sales manager at a dealership. In 2014, the war began, causing another financial crisis in Ukraine, and car sales dropped. At the same time, many people from the occupied territories and conflict zones moved into our region. I started working as a social worker at a homeless center, helping displaced people restore documents and find housing.

Later, I took responsibility for the facility operations at the homeless center and eventually became Deputy Director for administrative affairs. During that time, our city authorities decided to renovate a building for people who had lost their homes and to create a Social Hotel with support from the German government. I was given the opportunity to oversee the renovation, manage the installation of utilities, and coordinate the necessary documentation for commissioning the building.

During that period, a temporary ceasefire was established and the country's economy began to recover. After the Social Hotel became operational and the work was organized, I decided to return to the automotive business. I received an excellent offer from a Renault dealership, where I worked until I decided to leave Ukraine to reunite with my family, who had already relocated to the Netherlands.

During this time, I decided to learn a new profession and chose the IT field (HTML/CSS, basic JavaScript). I am motivated to continue learning new technologies and languages, such as React, Vue, Node, and I am also open to exploring other areas within IT.
